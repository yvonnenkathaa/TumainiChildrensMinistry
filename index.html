About Tumaini Children’s Ministry

Mission

To give hope and build families for children in need through helping them shape their futures and share in community development.

Vision

To ensure every child belongs to a family in a supportive environment and grows with love, respect and security.

History

P.C.E.A Tumaini children’s ministry comprises Tumaini and Huruma children’s home, an institution of Presbyterian Church of East Africa Riamukurwe Parish being part of the mission work addressing the needy persons especially the child welfare, including feeding, clothing, medical and education. These are children from normal homes experiencing economic, emotional, and social upheavals. The idea is to provide transient support to enable the children under those unfortunate circumstances achieve meaning in their lives particularly elementary and secondary education.

Since its foundation the ministry expanded from short-term parish outreach to a structured program delivering shelter, schooling, vocational skills training, and family strengthening. Through partners and parish governance Tumaini aims to prepare children for independent, productive lives while preserving their dignity and wellbeing.

Facilities

  • Children's dormitories (separate boys & girls units)
  • Dining hall and kitchen
  • Classrooms and study center
  • Skills training workshop (tailoring, plumbing, welding, electrical, ICT)
  • Playground and sports fields
  • Health & counseling room

Governance & Safeguarding

The children’s ministry is owned by PCEA Riamukurwe Parish. A Board of Management and Parish Session oversee governance. All staff sign a code of conduct and follow child protection policies; safeguarding procedures are implemented and available on request.

Programs

Shelter & Care

Daily routines, psychosocial support and safe dormitory living with trained caregivers.

Education Support

School fees, uniforms, tutoring, library access and exam preparation.

Vocational Skills

Tailoring, welding, plumbing, electrical and ICT training — with income-generation projects.

Health & Nutrition

Balanced meals from farm produce, routine checks and referrals to local clinics.

Family Strengthening

Tracing, counselling and reintegration where safe — target reintegration of at least five children per quarter.

Get Involved

There are several meaningful ways to support Tumaini. Below are clear steps, expectations, and how your involvement helps.

Volunteer

Short-term volunteers help with tutoring, activities and events. Long-term volunteers may support program coordination, administration or training. Volunteers attend a brief induction and child protection orientation before working with children.

Sponsor a Child

Sponsorship covers school fees, uniforms, basic medical care and pocket funds. Sponsors receive termly progress reports and (where appropriate) photos and updates.

Partner & Corporate Support

Local businesses, churches, and NGOs can partner on projects (workshop builds, vocational training, school fee drives). Partners receive impact reports and recognition in our communications.

What we ask from volunteers/partners

  • Commitment to child safeguarding and ministry rules
  • Attendance at orientation sessions
  • Respect for staff direction and community norms

Donate

Your donations support daily operations and long-term projects — education, nutrition, healthcare and skills training. We aim for transparency and send donors simple impact summaries.

Suggested Donation Uses

  • KES 2,000 / month — support a child's school essentials
  • KES 5,000 — monthly meals for the home
  • KES 15,000 — vocational workshop supplies (per term)
  • One-off donations — building repairs, mattresses, or medical emergencies
